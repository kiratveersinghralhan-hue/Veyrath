Policy / returns

Fair when something is wrong.

Made-after-order products have narrower change-of-mind options, but damage, defects, and incorrect fulfilment should be reported and reviewed.

Last updated: 30 June 2026 · Fulfilment storefront terms may also apply

1. Made-to-order limits

Because print-on-demand items are produced for a specific order, returns for change of mind, dislike of a selected colour, or an incorrectly chosen size may not be available. Please review product information and the fulfilment storefront’s size guide before payment.

2. Problems we ask you to report

Contact support if the item arrives damaged, has a material or print defect beyond normal minor production variation, or is not the product or size ordered. Report the issue as soon as reasonably possible—ideally within 3 days of delivery—so fulfilment records and carrier evidence remain useful.

3. What to include

  • Order reference and the email or phone used at checkout.
  • Clear photos of the entire item and close-ups of the problem.
  • Photos of the shipping label and packaging if damaged or incorrect.
  • A short description of when and how the issue was noticed.

4. Review and outcome

A report is reviewed against the order and the fulfilment partner’s policy. Depending on the evidence, availability, and applicable law, the outcome may be a replacement, reprint, refund, or another reasonable remedy. Do not send an item back without return instructions; an unapproved parcel may not reach the correct facility.

5. Wear, care, and variation

Damage from use, incorrect washing, alteration, accidental tearing, or ordinary wear is not a production defect. Small differences in placement or colour can occur between a digital mockup and a printed garment; a meaningful mismatch will be reviewed on its facts.

6. Refund timing

If a refund is approved, it is normally sent through the original payment route by the checkout or fulfilment provider. Banking and payment-network processing time is outside VEYRATH’s direct control.

7. Consumer rights

This policy does not remove rights that apply under Indian consumer law. Where an external storefront’s policy offers stronger protection, that stronger applicable term may be used.
